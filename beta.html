Digital Wayfinding Beta Test

You are invited! Please help us test a new tool helping people get to and from People Mover Stations.

Start somewhere downtown and use the tool to find your way to a People Mover Station. We have some scenarios below!

Complete the feedback survey to help us refine it for everyday users. We would like to understand what worked, what might be confusing, and what could be improved before wider release.

Suggested Test Scenarios

Try one or more of these real-life downtown situations, or use the tool during your own day downtown and tell us how it performs. As you test, please note anything that slows you down: confusing wording, unclear buttons, unexpected map behavior, wrong or surprising station suggestions, missing accessibility information, or directions that do not match what you see on the street.

1. Going to a Tigers game

You are going to a Tigers game at Comerica Park. Start from a lot, garage, or on the street somewhere downtown. Use the digital wayfinding tool to figure out the nearest station to board, which station to exit, and how to walk from the People Mover to Comerica Park.

2. Visiting Huntington Place

You are attending an event, conference, or meeting at Huntington Place. It's lunchtime, and you'd like to visit a restaurant near Library St. and Grand River. From the first floor inside Huntington Place, navigate to the Huntington Place People Mover Station and then figure out which station to ride to for your lunchtime destination.

3. Riverfront to Renaissance Center

You have just finished a stroll on the Riverwalk and have ended up on the eastern side of the Renaissance Center. Use the tool to navigate to the Renaissance Center People Mover Station, starting from near Atwater St. and Beaubien St.

4. Starting from Greektown

You are starting in Greektown after visiting a restaurant, casino, or entertainment spot. Use the tool to find the Greektown Station and navigate to another downtown destination. Pay attention to whether the station access information is helpful and whether anything feels confusing.

5. Getting from parking to a downtown destination

Park anywhere downtown — a garage, surface lot, hotel, workplace, or street space — and use the tool as if you were trying to avoid a longer walk. Choose a destination such as Campus Martius, Ford Field, Hart Plaza, Little Caesars Arena, or the Rosa Parks Transit Center and see whether the tool helps you make a clear plan.

6. Use it naturally for part of your day

If you are spending time downtown, use the tool whenever it seems helpful. Try it while walking between meetings, events, restaurants, parking, hotels, or transit connections. We want to know whether it feels useful in real life, not just whether it works in a controlled test.

How this tool may be used

People Mover is evaluating if this, or similar tools, would help residents, visitors, workers, and eventgoers get to People Mover stations and quickly understand how the People Mover can fit into their trip.

It would be accessed via QR codes at hotels, parking lots, garages, event venues, visitor centers, and other downtown locations.

Someone could scan the QR code after parking, leaving a hotel or event, or trying to reach a specific destination. The tool should help them answer two simple questions: “Where is the nearest station?” and “Which station should I use for where I’m going?”

What we're testing

We are looking for real-world feedback on whether the tool feels simple, accurate, and useful without needing extra explanation. Please pay attention to station recommendations, walking directions, entrance tips, accessibility notices, Apple Maps/Google Maps behavior, and anything that feels unclear.

You do not need to follow the scenarios perfectly. You can also use the tool during a normal day downtown and tell us where it helped, where it fell short, or where you expected something different to happen.
